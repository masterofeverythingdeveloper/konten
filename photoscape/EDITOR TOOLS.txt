<h2>Filter Tab</h2>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr valign="top">
<td><img src="http://img.photoscape.org/v005/batch/filter.jpg" /></td>
<td>
<div style="padding-left: 20px;"><strong>Auto Level</strong>: Redistributes colors throughout the photo in hopes of color correcting photos <br /><strong>Auto Contrast</strong>: Alters photo by changing the contrast between lighting and colors <br /><strong>Sharpen</strong>: Increases the appearance of edges in a photo <br /><strong>Backlight Correction</strong>: If percent is a (+), makes darker areas in the photo brighter; if percent is a (+/-), makes bright areas darker and dark areas brighter <br /><strong>Contrast Enhance</strong>: Alters photo by increasing the contrast between lighting and colors <br /><strong>Deepen</strong>: Makes the tones in the photo appear richer <br /><strong>Brighten</strong>: Makes the photo lighter <br /><strong>Darken</strong>: Makes the photo darker <br /><strong>Bright</strong>: Increase or decrease level of light and darkness in a photo <br /><strong>Contrast</strong>: Increase or decrease level of contrast between lighting and color in a photo <br /><strong>Decolor</strong>: Drains colors from a photo <br /><strong>Bloom</strong>: Applies a softer filter to the photo, making the edges bleed into each other <br /><strong>Film Effect</strong>: Makes photo appear to be taken on different kinds of film <br /><strong>Vignetting</strong>: Adds different gradients of darkness around the edges of the photo <br /><strong>Sepia</strong>: Makes the photo black and white and appear to be developed using Sepia toner (A different color than black and white, but still monochromatic) <br /><strong>Antique Photo</strong>: Gives the appearance of an older photograph <br /><strong>Grayscale</strong>: Makes the photo black and white <br /><strong>Blur</strong>: Blurs the photo <br /><strong>Negative</strong>: Gives the photo the appearance of a film negative <br /><strong>Erode</strong>: Strengthens colors in photo (opposite of dilating) <br /><strong>Dilate</strong>: Weakens colors in photo (opposite of eroding) </div>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
