<h1>Stop</h1>
</div>
<hr /></div>
<div class="content_block_text" dir="auto">
<h4>Use the <code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> parameter to finish a Job partway through the process. Stopping a Job at an earlier percentage can create blurrier, less detailed results.</h4>
<section class="infoBox">
<div class="title"></div>
<div class="content">
<p><code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> accepts values: 10&ndash;100.<br />The default <code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> value is 100.<br /><code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> does not work while <a href="https://docs.midjourney.com/upscalers">Upscaling</a>.</p>
</div>
</section>
<hr />

<h2 id="stops-affect-on-upscaling">Stops affect on Upscaling</h2>
<p>The <code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> parameter does not affect a Job while upscaling. However, stopping will produce a softer, less detailed initial image that will influence the level of detail in the final upscaled result. The upscaled images below used the <a href="https://docs.midjourney.com/v1/docs/upscalers">Beta Upscaler</a>.</p>

<h2 id="how-to-change-the-stop-percentage">How to Change the Stop Percentage</h2>
<h3 id="use-the-stop-parameter">Use the <code data-backticks="1">--stop</code> Parameter</h3>
<p>Add <code data-backticks="1">--stop &lt;value&gt;</code> to the end of your prompt.</p>
