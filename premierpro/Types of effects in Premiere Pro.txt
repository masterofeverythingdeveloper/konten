<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 21px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Explore the variety of audio and video effects that Premiere Pro offers. Also learn how to find and organize effects in the Effects panel.</p>

<div class="cmp-text " daa-im="true" daa-lh="In Article|Article body" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Premiere Pro includes various audio and video effects that you can apply to clips in your video program. An effect can add a special visual or audio characteristic or provide an unusual feature attribute. For example, an effect can alter the exposure or color of footage, manipulate sound, distort images, or add artistic effects. You can also use effects to rotate and animate a clip or adjust its size and position within the frame. You control the intensity of an effect by the values that you set for it. You can also animate the controls for most effects using keyframes in the Effect Controls panel or in a Timeline panel.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">You can create and apply presets for all effects. You can animate effects using keyframes and view information about individual keyframes directly in a Timeline panel.</p>
</div>

You can also use effects created by external manufacturers that you can use as a plug-in in Premiere Pro.&nbsp;</p>

<h2 id="fixed_effects" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; line-height: 1em; font-weight: 600; padding: 22px 0px 18px; margin: 0px; font-size: 24px;">Fixed effects</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; clear: none; padding: 0px; display: inline-block; width: 749.156px; font-size: 16px; float: left; color: #333333; font-family: adobe-clean, 'Source Sans Pro', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;">
<div class="cmp-text " daa-im="true" daa-lh="In Article|Article body" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Every clip you add to a Timeline panel has Fixed effects pre-applied, or built in. Fixed effects control the inherent properties of a clip and appear in the Effect Controls panel whenever the clip is selected. You can adjust all of the Fixed effects in the Effect Controls panel. However, the Program Monitor, Timeline panel, and Audio Mixer also provide controls that are often easier to use. The Fixed effects include the following:</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;"><strong style="font-weight: bolder; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">Motion</strong></p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Includes properties that allow you to animate, rotate, and scale your clips, adjust their anti-flicker property, or composite them with other clips. (To adjust the Motion effect in the Program Monitor, see<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/motion-position-scale-rotate-clip.html#adjust_position_scale_and_rotation" daa-level="4" daa-lh="8" daa-ll="Adjust position, scale, and rotation" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Adjust position, scale, and rotation</a><span>&nbsp;</span>and<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/motion-position-scale-rotate-clip.html#animate_motion_in_the_program_monitor" daa-level="4" daa-lh="9" daa-ll="Animate motion in the Program Monitor" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Animate motion in the Program Monitor</a>.)</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;"><strong style="font-weight: bolder; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">Opacity</strong></p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Lets you reduce the opacity of a clip for use in such effects as overlays, fades, and dissolves.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;"><strong style="font-weight: bolder; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">Time Remapping</strong></p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Lets you slow down, speed up, or reverse playback, or freeze a frame, for any part of a clip. Provides fine control for the acceleration or deceleration of these changes.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;"><strong style="font-weight: bolder; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">Volume</strong></p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Controls the volume for any clip that contains audio. (For information about adjusting the Volume effect, see<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/adjusting-volume-levels.html#adjust_track_volume_with_keyframes" daa-level="4" daa-lh="10" daa-ll="Adjust volume with keyframes" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Adjust volume with keyframes</a>,<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/adjusting-volume-levels.html#adjust_volume_in_effect_controls" daa-level="4" daa-lh="11" daa-ll="Adjust volume in Effect Controls" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Adjust volume in Effect Controls</a>,<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/adjusting-volume-levels.html#set_track_volume_in_the_audio_track_mixer" daa-level="4" daa-lh="12" daa-ll="Set track volume in the Audio Mixer" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Set track volume in the Audio Mixer</a>,<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/adjusting-volume-levels.html#normalize_one_or_more_clips" daa-level="4" daa-lh="13" daa-ll="Adjusting gain and volume, Normalize one or more clips" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Adjusting gain and volume, Normalize one or more clips</a>, and<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://helpx.adobe.com/id_en/premiere-pro/using/adjusting-volume-levels.html#normalize_the_master_track" daa-level="4" daa-lh="14" daa-ll="Normalize the Master track" style="background-color: transparent; border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #1473e6; text-decoration: none;">Normalize the Master track</a>.)</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Because Fixed effects are already built in to each clip, you need only adjust their properties to activate them.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Premiere Pro renders Fixed effects after any Standard effects that are applied to the clip. Standard effects are rendered in the order in which they appear, from the top down. You can change the order of Standard effects by dragging them to a new position in the Effect Controls panel, but you can&rsquo;t reorder Fixed effects.</p>

<h2 id="standard_effects" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; line-height: 1em; font-weight: 600; padding: 22px 0px 18px; margin: 0px; font-size: 24px;">Standard effects</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; clear: none; padding: 0px; display: inline-block; width: 749.156px; font-size: 16px; float: left; color: #333333; font-family: adobe-clean, 'Source Sans Pro', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;">
<div class="cmp-text " daa-im="true" daa-lh="In Article|Article body" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Standard effects are additional effects that you must first apply to a clip to create a desired result. You can apply any number or combination of Standard effects to any clip in a sequence. Use Standard effects to add special characteristics or to edit your video, such as adjusting tone or trimming pixels. Premiere Pro includes many video and audio effects, which are located in the Effects panel. Standard effects must be applied to a clip and then adjusted in the Effect Controls panel. Certain video effects allow direct manipulation using handles in the Program Monitor. All Standard effect properties can be animated over time using keyframing and changing the shape of the graphs in the Effect Controls panel. The smoothness or speed of the effect animation can be fine-tuned by adjusting the shape of Bezier curves in the Effect Controls panel.</p>

<h2 id="high_bit_depth_effects" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; line-height: 1em; font-weight: 600; padding: 22px 0px 18px; margin: 0px; font-size: 24px;">32-bit Color</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; clear: none; padding: 0px; display: inline-block; width: 749.156px; font-size: 16px; float: left; color: #333333; font-family: adobe-clean, 'Source Sans Pro', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;">
<div class="cmp-text " daa-im="true" daa-lh="In Article|Article body" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Premiere Pro includes some video effects and transitions that support high-bit-depth processing. When applied to high-bit-depth assets, such as v210-format video and 16-bit-per-channel (bpc) Photoshop files, these effects can be rendered with 32bpc pixels. The result is better color resolution and smoother color gradients with these assets than would be possible with the earlier standard 8 bit per channel pixels. A 32-bpc badge appears to the right of the effect name in the Effects panel for each high-bit-depth effect.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">To enable high-bit-depth rendering for these effects, select the Maximum Bit Depth video rendering option in the New Sequence dialog box.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; clear: none; padding: 0px; display: inline-block; width: 749.156px; font-size: 16px; float: left; color: #333333; font-family: adobe-clean, 'Source Sans Pro', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;">

<h2 id="yuv-effects" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; line-height: 1em; font-weight: 600; padding: 22px 0px 18px; margin: 0px; font-size: 24px;">YUV effects</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; clear: none; padding: 0px; display: inline-block; width: 749.156px; font-size: 16px; float: left; color: #333333; font-family: adobe-clean, 'Source Sans Pro', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif; font-style: normal; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; font-weight: 400; letter-spacing: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; white-space: normal; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; background-color: #ffffff; text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;">

<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">Effects in Premiere Pro that have the YUV logo do the processing directly on the YUV values without converting them to RGB first. The pixel values are never converted to RGB, and there is no unwanted color shifting.</p>
<p style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.5; margin: 10px 0px; overflow-wrap: break-word;">These effects make it easy for you to adjust contrast and exposure without shifting color.</p>
